'''Santa Maria del Carmine e Sant'Antonio''' was a small mid 19th century devotional chapel in what was the Via Pedacchia, most of which is now under the Via del Teatro di Marcello to the west of ''Il Vittoriano ''near Piazza Venezia. This is in the rione [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campitelli Campitelli].

The dedication was to [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Mount_Carmel Our Lady of Mount Carmel] and [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_of_Padua Anthony of Padua].
==Location==
The Via Pedacchia was a mediaeval street which started from the south-east corner of the Piazza di San Marco, then ran south, south-west and west to the former piazza with fountain which is now in the Via di San Venanzio. It was destroyed in the building of I[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altare_della_Patria l Vittoriano] in the late 1880's.

The site is under the western wing of that monument.
==Description==
This was described as a small but very well decorated chapel. It had a beautiful altar with a large picture of Our Lady of Mount Carmel, and a smaller one below of SS Anthony and [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philomena Philomena]. 

It does not appear on the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giambattista_Nolli#Nolli_Map Nolli map], and the devotion to St Philomena indicates a date of foundation in the mid19th century.
