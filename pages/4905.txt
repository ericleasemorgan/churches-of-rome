'''Santa Chiara al Centro di Produzione della RAI di Saxa Rubra '''is a late 20th century dependent chapel of the parish of [[Santa Maria Immacolata a Grottarossa]] , and is located at Largo Villy De Luca in [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saxa_Rubra Saxa Rubra], just south-east of the junction between the Via Flaminia and the [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autostrada_A90 Grande Raccordo Anulare] (Circonvallazione Settentrionale). This is in the [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grottarossa Grottarossa] suburban zone.

The dedication is to [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clare_of_Assisi St Clare], because she is the patron of everybody to do with television.

== History ==
This is the chapel for the ''[https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centro_radiotelevisivo_Biagio_Agnes Centro di Produzione della RAI]'' studio complex of the ''[https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rai Radiotelevisione Italiana]'', the Italian public television corporation. This was begun in 1987, and completed in 1990. The architect was [http://www.archpanella.it/ Roberto Panella]. 

The complex was renamed after [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biagio_Agnes Biagio Agnes] in 2011.

== Appearance ==
The chapel has no separate architectural identity, but has its own entrance.

The most important artwork here is an impressive and spectacular stained glass window depicting ''Mater Salvatoris ''by [https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giuseppe_Rogolino Giuseppe Rogolino]. A picture of it is [http://rogolino.it/mater-salvatoris/ here].

== Liturgy ==
The chapel is not served by the local parish clergy, but has its own chaplain. He is Msgr. Antonio Interguglielmi.

The liturgy here seems to be on an "as and when" basis, and is not public.
